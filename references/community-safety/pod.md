## Overview

Community safety focuses on violence that happens in public or shared spaces — gun violence, group-based violence, hate crimes, and threats in schools and workplaces. The St. Louis region has among the highest rates of gun violence in the United States, and both the city and county have invested in community violence intervention (CVI) programs that work alongside law enforcement.

This pod covers how to recognize threats, how to report them, what community-based programs exist to prevent violence, and how to respond when violence occurs.

---

## Key Concepts and Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Community violence** | Violence occurring in public or semi-public spaces between individuals who are not intimate partners or family members. Often involves firearms. |
| **Community Violence Intervention (CVI)** | Evidence-based programs that use credible messengers — often people with lived experience of violence — to interrupt cycles of violence and connect high-risk individuals to resources. |
| **Credible messenger** | A person who has lived experience similar to those they are trying to reach (often a formerly incarcerated person or former gang member) who can build trust and influence behavior. |
| **Gun violence** | Violence involving a firearm, including homicide, assault, suicide, and unintentional injury. Missouri has minimal gun regulation relative to national averages. |
| **Hate crime** | A criminal offense motivated in whole or in part by bias against a person's race, religion, national origin, ethnicity, sex, sexual orientation, disability, or other protected class. Under Missouri RSMo § 574.090. |
| **Bias incident** | An act that may be motivated by bias but does not rise to the level of a criminal offense. Still taken seriously and tracked. |
| **Active shooter / active threat** | An individual or group actively engaged in killing or attempting to kill people in a populated area. Response protocols: Run-Hide-Fight or ALICE. |
| **School safety** | A combination of physical security, threat assessment, mental health support, and restorative practices that contribute to safe learning environments. |
| **Red flag / extreme risk protection order (ERPO)** | A court order allowing temporary removal of firearms from a person who poses a danger. Missouri does not have a statewide ERPO law as of 2026. |
| **Gun relinquishment** | In cases involving domestic violence, federal law (the Lautenberg Amendment) prohibits persons convicted of DV misdemeanors from possessing firearms. Missouri courts can order relinquishment. |
| **Cure Violence** | A public health model for violence intervention that treats violence like an infectious disease and uses interrupters to break cycles of retaliation. |

## Gun Violence in the St. Louis Region

St. Louis City and County together represent one of the highest gun violence rates in the United States. Understanding the landscape:

- The majority of gun homicides involve people who know each other (not stranger violence).
- Most victims and perpetrators are young Black men, a disparity rooted in structural inequities.
- Gun suicides account for more than half of all gun deaths nationally.
- Illegal firearms trafficking from states with less restrictive laws (like Iowa and Kansas) contributes to gun availability.

### Missouri Firearms Laws (Key Points)

| Law / Statute | Description |
|---------------|-------------|
| RSMo § 571.030 | Unlawful use of weapons — list of prohibited conduct including firing into occupied areas |
| RSMo § 571.070 | Felons and certain others prohibited from possessing firearms |
| RSMo § 571.101–571.121 | Concealed carry permits and regulations |
| **No background check requirement** for private sales | Missouri does not require universal background checks for private firearm sales (unlike federal licensed dealers). |
| **No red flag / ERPO law** | Missouri has not enacted an extreme risk protection order law as of 2026. |
| **No waiting period** | Missouri does not impose a waiting period for firearm purchases. |
| **Permitless carry (Constitutional Carry)** | Missouri allows most adults to carry a concealed firearm without a permit (RSMo § 571.030). |

**For reporting illegal firearms or trafficking:**
- **ATF (Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco, Firearms and Explosives) St. Louis Field Division:** 314-269-3200
- **FBI St. Louis:** 314-231-4324
- **STL County Police:** 314-889-2341

## Community Violence Intervention Programs

### Cure Violence STL

Cure Violence is an evidence-based public health model that treats violence like a contagious disease. Trained "violence interrupters" — credible messengers with community ties — detect and intervene before shootings occur.

- **Approach:** Interrupt retaliatory violence, mediate conflicts, change social norms around violence
- **Target population:** Individuals at highest risk of being a victim or perpetrator of gun violence
- **Referrals:** Can be made by anyone — family, friends, community members, healthcare providers
- **STL Contact:** Contact via St. Louis County Health Department or local community organizations

### Other CVI Programs in STL Area

| Program | Description | Contact |
|---------|-------------|---------|
| **Alive and Well** | Addresses trauma from gun violence; hospital-based violence intervention | alivenstl.org |
| **Youth Violence Prevention Initiative (YVPI)** | STL County initiative connecting at-risk youth to services | Via STL County government |
| **Better Family Life** | Community-based organization providing CVI and youth services | 314-381-3900 |
| **St. Louis City Hospital Violence Intervention Program (HVIP)** | Connects gunshot wound patients to services at hospital bedside | Via Barnes-Jewish |

1. **If in progress:** Call **911**
2. **After the fact (non-emergency):** Call **314-889-2341** (STL County Police non-emergency)

## Hate Crimes and Bias Incidents

### What Is a Hate Crime in Missouri?

Under **RSMo § 574.090**, a hate crime (called "ethnic intimidation" in the statute) occurs when a person commits an underlying criminal offense (assault, property damage, threats, etc.) motivated by bias based on:
- Race
- Color
- Religion
- National origin or ancestry
- Sex
- Sexual orientation
- Gender identity
- Disability

A hate crime **adds a penalty** to the underlying offense — it is an enhancement, not a stand-alone charge.

### Reporting a Hate Crime

1. **Call 911** if the incident is ongoing or involves immediate physical danger.
2. **Call STL County Police non-emergency: 314-889-2341** and specifically state that you believe it is a hate crime.
3. **File with the FBI:** tips.fbi.gov — the FBI has civil rights jurisdiction over hate crimes.
4. **Contact the Missouri Attorney General's Office Civil Rights Unit:** 573-751-3321
5. **Contact the Anti-Defamation League (ADL) Midwest:** 314-432-6868 — they track and respond to bias incidents
6. **Document:** Photograph evidence, save written messages, note witnesses.

### Bias Incidents (Not Crimes)

A bias incident is an act motivated by prejudice that does not meet the legal definition of a crime (e.g., offensive graffiti that is quickly removed, a slur shouted at someone without accompanying assault). Steps:
- Report to local police for documentation
- Contact the ADL or NAACP St. Louis chapter
- Schools and workplaces have independent obligations to investigate bias incidents

## School Safety

### Missouri School Safety Framework

Missouri schools operate under:
- **Missouri Safe Schools Act (RSMo § 160.261):** Requires each school to have a written discipline policy, including reporting certain offenses to law enforcement.
- **RSMo § 160.665:** School Safety and Security Coordinator requirements.
- Each district must have a **Comprehensive School Safety Plan**.
- STL County schools participate in the **Missouri Center for Education Safety**.

### Active Threat Response: Run-Hide-Fight / ALICE

Most STL area schools use the **ALICE** protocol or similar:

- **Alert:** Be alerted to the threat
- **Lockdown:** Barricade the door if you cannot run
- **Inform:** Share information in real time
- **Counter:** As a last resort, use noise, distance, and distractors to disrupt the threat
- **Evacuate:** Get out if safe to do so

**Public response to active threat:**
1. **RUN** — If you can exit safely, leave immediately. Leave your belongings. Keep others with you if possible.
2. **HIDE** — If you cannot run, find a hiding place out of the shooter's view. Lock or barricade the door. Silence your phone.
3. **FIGHT** — As a last resort, act aggressively to disrupt the threat. Use objects as weapons. Commit to your actions.
4. **Call 911 when safe.** Tell them your location, number of people, and what you know about the threat.

### School Threat Reporting

- **Students can report threats anonymously:** Contact school administration or use the district's anonymous tip line.
- **Sandy Hook Promise Say Something app:** Allows students to anonymously report threats. Many STL area schools use this.
- **STL County Police School Resource Officers:** 314-889-2341
- **FBI tip line for school threats:** tips.fbi.gov

### Warning Signs of Student in Crisis (Threat Assessment)

Research shows that most school shooters give warnings before attacking. Warning signs include:
- Explicit or veiled threats of violence
- Preoccupation with weapons or past school shootings
- Drawing or writing about violence
- Giving away possessions
- Recent personal loss or humiliation
- Social isolation combined with grievance behavior

**If you see warning signs:** Report to a school counselor, administrator, or the anonymous tip line. Do not dismiss threats as "just talk."

## Gang Intervention

### Understanding Gangs in STL

"Gangs" in the St. Louis area are often loosely organized neighborhood groups rather than formal organizations. Violence tends to be retaliatory and block-by-block rather than organized criminal enterprise-driven.

### Resources for Gang-Involved Youth and Families

| Resource | Phone | Notes |
|----------|-------|-------|

| **Youth In Need** | 636-946-9927 | Youth crisis, shelter, outreach |
| **Covenant House Missouri** | 314-533-2241 | Youth ages 18–24, street outreach |
| **Urban League of Metropolitan St. Louis** | 314-615-3600 | Employment, youth programs |
| **St. Patrick Center** | 314-802-0700 | Employment, housing for high-risk adults |

**For young people trying to leave gang involvement:**
1. Talk to a trusted adult or a CVI outreach worker.
2. Contact Better Family Life (314-381-3900) — they have credible messengers who have left gang life.
3. Request a case manager through STL County Health Department or BHR (314-469-6644).
4. Consider relocation assistance if violence risk is high in current neighborhood.

## Step-by-Step Procedures

### Procedure: Responding to a Shooting in Your Community

1. **Call 911 immediately.** Give your location, describe the situation, and stay on the line.
2. **Get to safety.** Move away from the scene. Get behind a solid barrier if shots are still being fired.
3. **Do not return to the scene** until police declare it safe.
4. **If someone is injured:** Apply pressure to wounds if safe. Do not move the victim unless there is immediate threat.
5. **After the incident:** You may experience traumatic stress. Contact BHR (314-469-6644) or a mental health provider. See Behavioral Health Crisis pod.
6. **To report information:** Call Crime Stoppers at **866-371-TIPS** — you do not need to give your name.

### Procedure: Reporting a Suspected Hate Crime

1. **Document the incident** — take photos, save messages, write down what was said and by whom.
2. **Report to STL County Police:** 314-889-2341. Specifically say "I believe this is a hate crime."
3. **Request a case number** and ask whether a detective will be assigned.
4. **Report to the FBI** if the incident involves significant threat or appears organized: tips.fbi.gov or 314-231-4324.
5. **Contact the ADL, NAACP, or ACLU** for support and documentation of the broader pattern.
6. **Get support for yourself.** Hate crimes cause psychological harm. Contact BHR (314-469-6644) or a community organization.

## When to Escalate / Emergency Indicators

**Call 911 immediately if:**
- Shots are being fired
- A weapon is visible and a threat has been made
- A person has been physically assaulted
- An active threat is underway in a school or public building
- You witness a hate crime involving physical violence

**Escalate to FBI if:**
- The hate crime appears organized or connected to extremist groups
- The threat crosses jurisdictions
- The incident involves federal crimes (trafficking, terrorism)
