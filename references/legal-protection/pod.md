## Overview

The legal system offers several tools to protect people from violence and abuse. These include civil protective orders, victim rights during criminal proceedings, and financial compensation for crime victims. Many of these tools are available at no cost to victims. Legal aid organizations in St. Louis County can help people navigate the process, especially those who cannot afford an attorney.

This pod focuses on civil legal protections (protective orders, victim rights) and resources. For criminal law and statutes related to specific crimes, see the Violence & Abuse and Community Safety pods.

## Key Concepts and Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Order of Protection (OP)** | A civil court order that requires an abuser to stop harassing, threatening, or contacting the victim. Also called a restraining order or protective order. |
| **Ex Parte Order** | A temporary order issued without the respondent present. Granted immediately when the court finds "good cause." Valid until a full hearing (usually within 15 days). |
| **Full Order of Protection** | An order issued after both parties have been heard. Can last up to one year and be renewed. |
| **Child Order of Protection** | Filed on behalf of a minor child under Missouri's Child Protection Orders (RSMo § 455.500–455.538). |
| **Adult Abuse Act** | Missouri's primary civil domestic violence law (RSMo Chapter 455). Establishes orders of protection and related protections. |
| **Victim Rights** | Missouri law and the federal Crime Victims' Rights Act provide crime victims with specific rights during the criminal justice process. |
| **Crime Victim Compensation (CVC)** | State-funded financial assistance for crime victims to help cover costs related to the crime: medical care, counseling, lost wages, funeral expenses. |
| **VOCA (Victims of Crime Act)** | Federal law funding victim services programs nationwide, including Missouri's CVC program and local service providers. |
| **Mandatory reporter** | A person required by law to report suspected child abuse, elder abuse, or other abuse to authorities. |
| **Legal aid** | Free or low-cost civil legal services for people who cannot afford an attorney. |
| **VINE (Victim Information and Notification Everyday)** | A free automated service that notifies registered victims when an offender's custody status changes. |

## Protective Orders in Missouri

### Types of Orders

| Type | Who Can File | Duration | Hearing Required? |
|------|-------------|----------|-------------------|
| **Ex Parte (Temporary)** | Adult victim, on behalf of a child | Until full hearing (max 15 days) | No — judge decides based on petition alone |
| **Full Order of Protection** | Adult victim | Up to 1 year; renewable | Yes — both parties notified |
| **Child Protection Order** | Parent or guardian on behalf of a child | Up to 1 year; renewable | Yes for full order |
| **Renewal / Extension** | Petitioner | Can be renewed before expiration | Court review required |

### Who Can Get a Protective Order in Missouri

Under the **Missouri Adult Abuse Act (RSMo Chapter 455)**, you can file for an order of protection if you have been subjected to abuse by:
- A current or former spouse or domestic partner
- A family member (blood or marriage)
- A person you have a child with
- A person you have lived with

**For stalking or harassment by someone who does not fall into these categories:** File under RSMo § 455.010's harassment provisions or consult legal aid about other civil remedies.

### How to File a Protective Order in St. Louis County

**Step 1: Go to the courthouse**

> **St. Louis County Circuit Court — Family Court Division**
> 7900 Carondelet Ave, Clayton, MO 63105
> Phone: 314-615-8000
> Hours: Monday–Friday, 8:00 AM – 5:00 PM
> After-hours emergency orders: Contact STL County Police (314-889-2341); some law enforcement can facilitate emergency ex parte orders.

**Step 2: Request the forms**
- Ask the court clerk for the **Petition for Order of Protection** (Form AV-I or equivalent).
- Forms are also available at: courts.mo.gov

**Step 3: Complete the petition**
- Describe the abuse in specific, factual terms: what happened, when, where, any injuries, any witnesses.
- List all household members and children who should be included.
- Do not minimize — describe the most serious incidents.

**Step 4: File with the clerk**
- **Filing fee:** Waived for domestic violence petitioners under RSMo § 455.040. You should not have to pay.
- The clerk will give the petition to a judge.
- The judge reviews the petition the same day in most cases.

**Step 5: Receive the ex parte order**
- If granted, the ex parte order is effective immediately.
- The order will schedule a full hearing within 15 days.

**Step 6: Service of process**
- The respondent must be served before the full hearing.
- Law enforcement (STL County Sheriff or local police) serves the order.
- You do not contact the respondent.

**Step 7: Full hearing**
- Attend the hearing on the scheduled date. Bring:
  - Any evidence of abuse (photos, texts, medical records)
  - Witnesses if available
  - A support person (victim advocate, friend, family member)
- You can have a legal advocate or attorney present.

**Step 8: Full order issued**
- If granted, the full order can last up to 1 year and be renewed.
- The order is entered into the statewide domestic violence database and is enforceable by all law enforcement agencies.

### What a Protective Order Can Do

A protective order can require the respondent to:
- Stay away from the petitioner's home, workplace, school, and other locations
- Have no contact by any means (phone, text, email, social media, third parties)
- Leave a shared home
- Surrender firearms and ammunition (when DV is involved — federal Lautenberg Amendment)
- Pay temporary financial support
- Attend a batterers' intervention program

### What a Protective Order Cannot Do

- Guarantee safety — a piece of paper does not stop a determined abuser
- Resolve custody permanently (a separate family court process is needed)
- Automatically result in arrest (police must be called; violation of the order is a criminal offense)

### Violating a Protective Order

Violation of a protective order is a **Class A misdemeanor** in Missouri (RSMo § 455.085). A second violation is a **Class D felony**. If someone violates your order:
1. Call **911** immediately. Tell them the order is being violated.
2. Have a copy of the order with you if possible.
3. Ask police to arrest the respondent — they have the authority to do so.

## Victim Rights in Missouri

### Missouri Crime Victims' Rights Act (RSMo § 595.209)

Missouri's Crime Victims' Rights Act gives victims of violent crimes the right to:

- **Notification:** Be informed of proceedings, hearings, charges, pleas, verdicts, and sentences
- **Presence:** Attend all public court proceedings
- **Input:** Submit a victim impact statement before sentencing
- **Protection:** Be reasonably protected from the defendant during proceedings
- **Privacy:** Certain personal information kept confidential
- **Prompt resolution:** Have proceedings conducted with reasonable speed
- **Restitution:** Have the court order the defendant to pay restitution for losses
- **Information:** Be informed of the final disposition of the case
- **VINE notification:** Be notified when the offender is released or transferred

### How to Enforce Your Rights

- **Prosecutor's Victim-Witness Unit:** Contact the St. Louis County Prosecuting Attorney's Office Victim Services Unit: **314-615-2600**
- **Missouri Attorney General Victim Services:** ag.mo.gov/victims | 573-751-3321
- **Mo. Crime Victims Rights Enforcement:** If your rights are being violated, you may petition the court for enforcement.

### VINE (Victim Information and Notification Everyday)

- **Free registration:** Register at vinelink.com or call **1-800-811-0645**
- Receive automatic notifications when the offender is released, transferred, or escapes
- Available 24/7

## Crime Victim Compensation (CVC)

Missouri's Crime Victim Compensation Program provides financial assistance to victims of violent crime who have suffered financial hardship as a direct result of the crime.

### What CVC Can Cover

- Medical and dental bills
- Mental health counseling
- Lost wages (if injured and unable to work)
- Funeral and burial expenses
- Mileage to medical appointments
- Home security upgrades (in some cases)

### What CVC Does Not Cover

- Property damage or theft
- Pain and suffering
- Bills paid by insurance or Medicaid

### Eligibility Requirements

- The crime must have occurred in Missouri (or a Missouri resident victimized in a state with no CVC program).
- The crime must be a violent crime (assault, domestic violence, sexual assault, robbery, homicide, stalking, trafficking).
- The victim must have reported the crime to law enforcement within **48 hours** (exceptions exist for good cause, children, and sexual assault).
- The victim must cooperate with law enforcement and prosecution.
- The application must be filed within **2 years** of the crime.

### How to Apply

1. **Download the application:** ago.mo.gov/civil-division/crime-victims-compensation or call **573-526-6006**
2. Submit to: **Missouri Crime Victim Compensation Program, P.O. Box 210, Jefferson City, MO 65102**
3. Include: police report number, medical bills and records, documentation of lost wages
4. **Victim advocates** at local DV and sexual assault agencies can help complete the application — contact ALIVE (314-531-2003) or YWCA Metro St. Louis (314-531-3980).

**CVC Maximum Awards:** Up to $25,000 per claim (higher for catastrophic injury).

## Mandatory Reporting in Missouri

### Child Abuse and Neglect (RSMo § 210.115)

**Mandatory reporters must report** suspected child abuse or neglect **immediately** upon having reasonable cause to suspect it.

**Who is a mandatory reporter of child abuse in Missouri:**
- Healthcare professionals (physicians, nurses, dentists, EMTs, chiropractors)
- Mental health professionals (therapists, counselors, psychologists)
- Teachers, school counselors, administrators, coaches
- Social workers and case managers
- Child care workers, foster parents
- Law enforcement
- Clergy (with some exceptions)
- Juvenile officers
- Commercial film processors (for CSAM)
- Court-appointed special advocates (CASAs)
- Any person in a position of authority over children

**How to report:** **Missouri Child Abuse and Neglect Hotline: 1-800-392-3738** (24/7)

**Good faith immunity:** Mandatory reporters who report in good faith are immune from civil and criminal liability even if the report is not confirmed.

**Failure to report:** Knowing failure to report is a **Class A misdemeanor** in Missouri (RSMo § 210.165).

### Elder Abuse (RSMo § 192.2400)

**Mandatory reporters of elder abuse include:**
- Physicians and other healthcare professionals
- Mental health professionals
- Home health aides and personal care attendants
- Adult day care staff
- Law enforcement

**How to report:** **Missouri Adult Abuse and Neglect Hotline: 1-800-392-0210** (24/7)

### Reporting Requirements Summary

| Type | Hotline | Statute |
|------|---------|---------|
| Child abuse/neglect | 1-800-392-3738 | RSMo § 210.115 |
| Elder abuse/neglect | 1-800-392-0210 | RSMo § 192.2400 |
| Abuse in care facilities | 1-800-392-0210 | RSMo § 198.070 |
| Human trafficking (minors) | 1-800-392-3738 (Children's Division) | RSMo § 210.110 |

## Step-by-Step Procedures

### Procedure: Getting an Emergency Protective Order After Hours

1. Call **911** or STL County Police non-emergency **314-889-2341**.
2. Report the incident or threat and ask about an emergency protective order.
3. In Missouri, police can facilitate ex parte orders in some circumstances after hours.
4. If the court is open the next business day, go to **St. Louis County Circuit Court, 7900 Carondelet Ave, Clayton** first thing in the morning.
5. Contact **LSEM (314-534-4200)** for legal guidance.

### Procedure: Applying for Crime Victim Compensation

1. Report the crime to police as soon as possible (within 48 hours is preferred).
2. Get the police report number.
3. Collect documentation: medical bills, receipts, employer letter about lost wages.
4. Download or request the CVC application: **ago.mo.gov** or call **573-526-6006**.
5. Have a victim advocate review your application before submitting.
6. Submit by mail or through a local victim services agency.
7. The state will notify you of the decision in writing; you can appeal if denied.

### Procedure: Registering with VINE for Offender Notification

1. Go to **vinelink.com** or call **1-800-811-0645**.
2. Select Missouri and enter the offender's name or ID.
3. Register your phone number or email.
4. You will receive automatic calls or messages when the offender's status changes.
5. Update your contact information if you move or change your number.

## When to Escalate / Emergency Indicators

**Seek emergency legal help immediately if:**
- You are in physical danger right now → Call **911**
- An order of protection is being violated → Call **911**; say the order is being violated
- You are being threatened with a weapon
- A child is in immediate danger

**Seek urgent (same-day) legal help if:**
- You need an ex parte order before a threatened abuser returns
- You have just been assaulted and need to preserve legal options
- You were forced to sign financial documents or give up property

**Seek legal aid within a few days if:**
- You want to file for a full order of protection
- You need to understand your victim rights in a pending criminal case
- You need help applying for crime victim compensation
- You need help with a custody or housing issue related to violence
