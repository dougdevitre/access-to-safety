# Personal Safety Plan

> **AI Instructions:** When a user asks to build or fill out a safety plan, walk them through
> each section one at a time. Ask open-ended questions to draw out the information they want
> to record. Never pressure them to fill in sections they are not ready to complete. Remind
> them at the start that this document is for their eyes only and should be stored somewhere
> private — not in a shared device or account. Do not store, repeat back, or summarize
> personal identifying information beyond what the user explicitly writes into the plan.
> If the user is in immediate danger, pause the plan and direct them to call 911 or their
> local emergency number. After the plan is complete, encourage them to review and update it
> regularly and to share it with one trusted person if safe to do so.

---

## How to Use This Plan

This safety plan is a living document. Fill in as much or as little as you are comfortable
with. You do not need to complete every section in one sitting. Return to it whenever your
situation changes. Keep it somewhere only you can access — printed and hidden, or in a
password-protected app on a device your abuser cannot reach.

**No personally identifying information is required.** Use code words, initials, or
descriptions wherever it feels safer to do so.

## Section 4 — Safety in Your Own Home

If you have already left or your partner no longer lives with you, these steps help secure
your space.

- [ ] Change all locks (doors, windows, garage)
- [ ] Add a door reinforcement bar or door alarm
- [ ] Notify building management / landlord of the situation
- [ ] Ask neighbors to call police if they see my abuser near the building
- [ ] Change the security code for the building or alarm system
- [ ] Change Wi-Fi password and check for unknown devices on the network
- [ ] Install a peephole or video doorbell if I do not already have one
- [ ] Have a charged phone available at all times
- [ ] Keep doors and windows locked even when I am home
- [ ] Vary my routines so I am less predictable

## Section 8 — Safety and Technology / Digital Security

Technology is frequently used as a tool of abuse and surveillance. Take these steps.

**Devices and accounts:**

- [ ] I have changed passwords on all accounts (email, social media, banking, phone)
- [ ] I use a password manager or unique strong passwords my abuser does not know
- [ ] I have enabled two-factor authentication using a phone or email they cannot access
- [ ] I have checked my devices for tracking apps (see resources for how-to guides)
- [ ] I use a private browsing window when researching safety resources
- [ ] I clear my browser history after sensitive searches
- [ ] I have reviewed who has access to shared location services (Google Maps, Find My, etc.)
- [ ] I have removed my abuser from shared accounts (streaming, cloud storage, etc.)

**If I need a safer device:**

- Local DV organizations often provide safe phones — contact a local hotline to ask
- Public library computers and phones can be used without being traced to your device

## Section 9 — Go Bag Checklist

A go bag is a bag packed in advance that you can grab and leave with quickly. Store it
somewhere accessible but not obvious — a trusted friend's house, a locker, or a hidden spot.

**Documents (originals or copies):**

- [ ] Photo ID (driver's license, passport, state ID)
- [ ] Birth certificates (mine and children's)
- [ ] Social Security cards
- [ ] Immigration documents (visa, green card, passport)
- [ ] Marriage / divorce certificate
- [ ] Custody orders or protective orders
- [ ] Lease or mortgage documents
- [ ] Car title and registration
- [ ] Insurance cards (health, car, renters/home)
- [ ] Medical records and prescription list
- [ ] School records for children

**Money and access:**

- [ ] Cash (small bills)
- [ ] Debit or credit card in my name only
- [ ] Checks from my bank account

**Medications and health:**

- [ ] Prescription medications (at least a week's supply)
- [ ] Glasses, hearing aids, or other medical equipment
- [ ] First aid basics

**Communication:**

- [ ] Phone charger
- [ ] Prepaid / burner phone (optional)
- [ ] Written list of important phone numbers

**Personal items:**

- [ ] Keys (house, car, safe deposit box)
- [ ] Change of clothes for me (and children)
- [ ] Comfort items for children (small toy, book)
- [ ] Items of sentimental value that cannot be replaced
